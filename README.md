## Problem Creater's Organization
Department of Higher Education, Government of Punjab

Smart Education

## Problem Description
### Problem Statement
•	Despite the rising urgency of climate change and environmental degradation, environmental education remains largely theoretical in many Indian schools and colleges. Students are often taught textbook-based content with little emphasis on real-world application, local ecological issues, or personal responsibility.

•	There is a lack of engaging tools that motivate students to adopt eco-friendly practices or understand the direct consequences of their lifestyle choices. Traditional methods fail to instill sustainable habits or inspire youth participation in local environmental efforts.

### Impact
•	As future decision-makers, students must be environmentally literate and empowered to take meaningful actions. Without innovative education methods, we risk raising a generation unaware of sustainability challenges.

•	An interactive, practical approach to environmental learning will foster long-term behavioral change, local involvement, and a ripple effect across families and communities. This aligns with India's SDG goals and NEP 2020's emphasis on experiential learning.

### Expected Outcomes
•	A gamified mobile/web platform or app that teaches students about environmental issues through interactive lessons, challenges, quizzes, and real-world tasks (e.g., tree-planting, waste segregation).

•	Tracking of eco-points, enabling school-level competitions.

•	Rewards for sustainable practices through digital badges and recognition.

## Research and References
Details / Links of the Reference and Research Work

The proposed solution is based on various research studies and existing technologies in the fields of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs), and Transformers.

1. Research on GCN and Transformer Integration

A study titled “Transformer and Graph Convolutional Network for Text Classification” explains how combining GCN with transformers improves semantic understanding and accuracy in text classification tasks.

The research shows that transformers help capture sequential information, while GCN captures relationships between data points, making the model more effective.

2. Transformer-Based Graph Models

Research on Transformer-based Graph Convolutional Networks for Sentiment Analysis introduces hybrid models that can learn complex relationships between nodes and improve classification performance.

These models use attention mechanisms to identify important connections, even between indirectly related nodes.

3. Comparative Studies (Transformers vs GNNs)

A comparative study on fake news detection shows that transformer-based models achieve higher accuracy (above 85–98%) compared to GCN-based models.

However, GNNs are still useful for handling structured graph data efficiently.

4. Graph Transformer Advancements

Research on graph transformer networks highlights how transformers can be extended to graph data by incorporating structural relationships and positional encoding.

This improves the ability to process both sequential and relational data simultaneously.

5. Key Insights from Research

From the above studies, the following conclusions are derived:

Combining GCN + Transformer improves accuracy and representation learning

Attention mechanisms help capture long-range dependencies

Hybrid models are more effective for complex real-world problems

These approaches are widely used in text classification, recommendation systems, and prediction tasks
